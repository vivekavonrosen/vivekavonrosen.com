Keynote 01

The Authority Gap

Diagnostic · Roadmap · 45–60 minutes

Why the most qualified expert in the room rarely gets the stage — and how to architect the authority that changes that.

Every year, thousands of the most experienced women in professional services become less visible while building more expertise. The cost of that invisibility is no longer a career inconvenience. It is the difference between a referral business and a referenced one.

Drawing on her work with fractional CMOs, neuroscientists, rocket scientists, real estate trainers, and authors with deep business and industry expertise, Viveka introduces The Authority Gap Model™ — a four-gap diagnostic that maps the specific structural breakdowns between where an established expert currently sits and where her credibility should already be working for her.

The audience leaves with a precise diagnosis of where their own Visibility Infrastructure is breaking down, and a concrete roadmap to fix it — without performing online, starting over, or becoming someone they don't recognize.

AUDIENCE TAKEAWAYS

  • The four specific gaps that keep accomplished experts invisible
  • How to know which gap is costing you the most right now
  • Why the wrong order of building visibility produces worse results than no effort at all
  • What "fixed" looks like — the architectural standard, not the cosmetic one

BEST FIT FOR

  • Women's leadership conferences
  • Professional associations
  • Executive women's groups
  • Founder and consulting communities
  • Speaker bureaus serving senior expert women
Keynote 02

The Visibility Architecture Stack

Systems · Infrastructure · 45–60 minutes

Why the most disciplined experts are not the most visible — and what they're building that everyone else is just performing.

Visibility is not a behavior. It is a system. The women who have sustainable visibility are not more disciplined. They have built infrastructure that produces visibility without requiring the same effort every time.

This keynote introduces The Visibility Architecture Stack™ — a three-layer model that explains why most established experts post more and get less, and the correct sequence for building visible authority that compounds: Positioning, Infrastructure, Amplification. In that order. Every time.

Most experts and the people who advise them go straight to Layer 3 — content, podcasts, speaking, PR — without building Layers 1 and 2. The result is attention with nowhere to land. Viveka shows audiences the architecture underneath sustainable authority, and the specific reason hustle keeps failing where systems succeed.

AUDIENCE TAKEAWAYS

  • The three layers of a working visibility system, and why the order is non-negotiable
  • What "infrastructure" actually means for a one-person expertise business
  • How to evaluate whether your current visibility work is structurally sound or cosmetically active
  • A specific test for whether your LinkedIn presence is built to a speaking-inquiry standard

BEST FIT FOR

  • Founder and entrepreneur conferences
  • Coach, consultant, and advisor audiences
  • Personal branding and marketing events
  • Author and speaker associations
  • Sales and business development teams
Keynote 03

The Authority Engine

Contrarian AI · Practical · 45–60 minutes

The established experts most likely to be overtaken by AI are not the ones using it wrong. They are the ones waiting until they understand it better.

AI is not replacing twenty-year expertise. It is amplifying it. The expert with a documented methodology and an AI system trained to extend her voice is not competing with AI. She is the one everyone else is competing with.

This keynote introduces The Authority Engine™ — Viveka's proprietary system for using AI not as a content generator but as a strategic extension of an expert's voice, judgment, and intellectual property. She walks the audience through how she uses it in her own business, where it has surprised her, and where she has had to override it.

This is not a tools talk. It is a positioning talk for a moment where most senior professionals are quietly afraid they are about to be made redundant, and most AI evangelists are quietly afraid they will be exposed as having no underlying expertise. Viveka names what neither side is willing to say out loud, and gives the audience a working model for the integration that actually wins.

AUDIENCE TAKEAWAYS

  • Why generic AI hurts established experts more than it helps them
  • The difference between AI as content generator and AI as voice extension
  • What "well-trained AI" actually means for a 20-year practitioner
  • How to use AI visibly as a credibility signal — not invisibly as a shortcut

BEST FIT FOR

  • Corporate leadership events
  • Future-of-work and innovation conferences
  • Professional services and consulting firms
  • Senior women in technology programs
  • Association leadership tracks

Long Bio · For Programs & Press

Viveka von Rosen is a LinkedIn Expert and AI Strategist for Women Founders. She works with coaches, consultants, trainers, advisors, authors, and speakers — women with deep business and industry expertise who have built real businesses and want the visibility, voice, and systems to grow them.

She has been on LinkedIn for 21 years. In 2006, she founded Linked Into Business, a LinkedIn training and consulting firm. In 2017, she co-founded Vengreso, which became one of the world's largest digital sales transformation companies before pivoting into productivity software in 2022. She walked away from Vengreso to build something more aligned — a coaching, consulting, and speaking practice grounded in her proprietary Visibility Infrastructure OS.

Viveka is the bestselling author of LinkedIn Marketing: An Hour a Day, along with 101 Ways to Rock LinkedIn, 101 Ways to Rock Change, Linked Insecurity: Who's Watching You, and The Countess Code Journals. She is also a LinkedIn Learning and Lynda.com author — chosen by LinkedIn itself to teach the platform she has been studying for two decades. Her work has been featured in Forbes, Fast Company, Money Magazine, the Miami Herald, the Denver Post, and Thrillist. She has spoken to audiences from Auckland to Anchorage for organizations including Inbound, NATO/OTAN, SHRM, Oracle, Amazon, the National Speakers Association, Social Media Marketing World, Content Marketing World, and MarketingProfs.

She lives in Loveland, Colorado, runs the Women's Technology Integration Collaborative, and is the architect of the Authority Gap Model™, the Visibility Architecture Stack™, and the Authority Engine™ — three frameworks she uses with private clients and brings to the stage.
