The part most "AI for beginners" stuff gets wrong

You're not a 25-year-old engineer. You're also not a beginner.

Here's the situation a lot of accomplished women are in right now, and I'd bet it's at least a little familiar: AI showed up fast, everyone suddenly has an opinion, and most of what's out there assumes you're either a 25-year-old engineer or someone who's never once opened ChatGPT — when you're sitting in the wide space those two extremes never account for, which is to say you're smart, experienced, busy, and entirely capable of learning this, you just don't have the time or the patience to wade through forty YouTube tutorials made for somebody else's life.

And the quieter fear underneath all of that — the one I hear a lot — is that the moment you hand your work to AI, you'll get back something beige and voiceless that sounds like everyone else, because that's exactly what happens when you let the tool drive.

Which is the whole reason WTIC exists — in here, you will use AI to strengthen, not dull, your voice. We use tools like AI, Agents, LinkedIn, Substack, video, and imagery to scale what only you know. Shine your light — online.

What you actually get when you're inside

Part classroom, part workshop, all in good company

WTIC is part classroom and part workshop, with the kind of group chat you wish you'd had the last time something broke at 9pm and you didn't know who to ask. Inside you'll find curated trainings that skip the filler, prompt libraries you can actually use, GPT tools, and a community of women who'll answer your question instead of making you feel like you should already know the answer.

The goal in here is always integration — getting these tools into your real work, the client intake form, the content system, the website, the little app you've been describing to people for two years now, so your expertise scales instead of staying stuck in your head and your calendar.

That's the kind of intelligent leverage I actually believe in — more out of what you already know, at a pace that doesn't cost you your evenings.

How it works — the monthly rhythm

Two live sessions a month, on the calendar

Same time, every month, so you can plan your life around them instead of the other way around.

Second Tuesday

Expert Trainings

A real teaching session on whatever tool or system or build actually matters for your business right now — the actual how, not forty minutes of theory you'll forget by lunch.

Fourth Tuesday

Integration Labs

This is where you bring what you're working on and we apply it together — your project, your stuck point, your half-finished thing. The labs are where the watching turns into something you've actually built.

Everything's recorded and lives in the library, so if a session lands at a bad time, you watch the replay whenever it actually fits your week. No one's grading you. There's no behind.

Who this is for (and who it isn't)

An honest fit check

This is for you if you've got real expertise — years of it — you're done waiting around for someone to hand you permission to use these tools, you'd genuinely rather build things you own than rent your whole business from platforms that change the rules every quarter, and you'd take learning alongside other smart women over sitting alone at midnight with thirty browser tabs open any day of the week.

It's probably not for you if you want someone to do all of it for you and never actually touch the tools yourself, or if you're hunting for a get-rich-quick AI side hustle — that's a different room, and honestly, a different person running it.

Questions you're probably asking

The honest answers

Do I need to be technical?

No. That's sort of the whole point of the room.

I've already built things in ChatGPT — is this redundant?

Not at all, and walking away from what you've already made would be a waste — we pick it up and take it further instead of starting you over from zero.

How much time does this actually take?

Two live sessions a month, both recorded. Show up to what you can, watch the rest whenever it fits.

Is the free tier genuinely useful, or just a tease?

Genuinely useful — there's real training and real conversation in there. The live building and the hands-on support are what you get in Pro.

What if I fall behind?

There's no behind. It's a library and a community, not a course with a deadline breathing down your neck.

Can I cancel anytime?

The monthly plan, yes — cancel whenever you like, and you keep access through the end of the month you've already paid for.
